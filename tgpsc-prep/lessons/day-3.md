## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **The Preamble and its Keywords**

The Preamble is the Constitution compressed into one paragraph — and TGPSC tests every word, comma and case linked to it.

**Anatomy of the Preamble:**

| Component | Content |
|---|---|
| Source of authority | "**WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA**" — popular sovereignty |
| Nature of the state | **Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic** |
| Objectives | **Justice** (social, economic, political) · **Liberty** (thought, expression, belief, faith, worship) · **Equality** (status, opportunity) · **Fraternity** (dignity of the individual + unity & integrity of the nation) |
| Date of adoption | `26 November 1949` (the date written in the Preamble itself) |

**Keyword meanings (one line each):**

1. **Sovereign** — no external authority above India.
2. **Socialist** — added by the `42nd Amendment (1976)`; India follows *democratic* socialism (mixed economy).
3. **Secular** — also added in `1976`; India's model = **equal respect to all religions** (positive secularism), not strict wall-of-separation.
4. **Democratic** — authority from elections; covers political, social and economic democracy.
5. **Republic** — elected head of state (President), not hereditary; public offices open to all.
6. `42nd Amendment` also changed "unity of the Nation" → "**unity and integrity** of the Nation".

**The Preamble in court:**

| Case | Holding |
|---|---|
| **Berubari Union (1960)** | Preamble is **not** a part of the Constitution |
| **Kesavananda Bharati (1973)** | Overruled Berubari — Preamble **is** a part; can be amended, but not its **basic features** |
| **LIC of India (1995)** | Reaffirmed: Preamble is an integral part |

- The Preamble is **non-justiciable** (not enforceable in court), is **neither a source of power nor a limitation**, and has been **amended only once** (1976).

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — Order of the five adjectives: **So-So-Se-De-Re** — Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic. Examiners jumble the order to trap you.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Objectives in sequence "**JLEF**": **J**ustice → **L**iberty → **E**quality → **F**raternity ("Justice Loves Equal Friends").

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — 'Socialist', 'Secular', 'Integrity' were **not** in the original 1949 Preamble — all three arrived together via the `42nd Amendment, 1976`. 'Republic' and 'Democratic' were always there.

**💡 Example:** K.M. Munshi called the Preamble the "**political horoscope**" of the Constitution; Thakurdas Bhargava its "**soul**"; N.A. Palkhivala its "**identity card**" — quote-attribution MCQs come straight from these.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Preamble is based on the **Objectives Resolution** (Nehru, `13 Dec 1946`).
- Justice order: **social → economic → political**; Liberty order: **thought → expression → belief → faith → worship**.
- Fraternity assures **dignity of the individual** and **unity & integrity of the nation**.
- Ideals borrowed: Liberty-Equality-Fraternity from the **French Revolution**; Justice trio inspired by the **Russian Revolution**.
- Preamble can be invoked to interpret ambiguous articles — its legal use.
- Amended once: `42nd (1976)`.

## 📘 Subject 2: Telangana Schemes & Important Incidents

### Let's learn about **Rythu Bharosa, Indiramma Atmiya Bharosa & the ₹2 Lakh Loan Waiver**

The farm package is the costliest guarantee — and its numbers, dates and design changes are prime TGPSC material.

**Rythu Bharosa (investment support):**

| Feature | Rythu Bandhu (old, BRS) | **Rythu Bharosa (new, Congress)** |
|---|---|---|
| Amount | `₹10,000`/acre/year | **`₹12,000`/acre/year** |
| Instalments | 2 (Vaanakalam + Yasangi) | 2 × `₹6,000` per season |
| Rollout | From 2018 | From **Sankranti, January 2025** |
| Coverage change | All landowners | Cultivable land focus — non-agricultural land excluded (*verify latest official norms*) |

**Indiramma Atmiya Bharosa:**
- The missing piece Rythu Bandhu never had — **`₹12,000` per year to landless agricultural labourer families** (eligibility linked to NREGS work-days — *verify latest official figure*).
- Launched alongside Rythu Bharosa in the January 2025 package, with the new fine-rice (Sanna Biyyam) ration rollout the same season.

**The ₹2 lakh crop loan waiver (2024):**

1. Promise: waive crop loans up to **`₹2 lakh`** per farmer family.
2. Executed in **three tranches** (up to `₹1 lakh` → `₹1.5 lakh` → `₹2 lakh`) during **July–August 2024**.
3. Scale: lakhs of farmer accounts; outlay in tens of thousands of crores (*verify latest official figure*).
4. Ration-card/family definition disputes over eligibility made news — an "important incident" angle.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — Support went **up** (`₹10k → ₹12k`) *and* coverage went **wider** (landless labourers included via Atmiya Bharosa) — remember both halves of the reform.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**12-12-2**": ₹**12**k/acre for farmers, ₹**12**k/year for landless, ₹**2** lakh loan waiver.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Rythu **Bharosa** (Telangana, per-acre) ≠ **PM-KISAN** (Central, `₹6,000` flat per family) ≠ Rythu **Bandhu** (the old scheme). Three different schemes, three different designs.

**💡 Example:** A tenant family owning nothing but labour got `₹0` under Rythu Bandhu; under the new package the same family draws `₹12,000` as Atmiya Bharosa — while a 3-acre owner-cultivator gets `₹36,000` a year plus a one-time loan waiver up to `₹2 lakh`. Design-comparison questions love this contrast.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Rythu Bharosa = `₹12,000`/acre/year, two seasonal instalments, launched Sankranti 2025.
- Atmiya Bharosa = landless farm-labour families, `₹12,000`/year.
- Loan waiver: up to `₹2 lakh`, three tranches, Jul–Aug 2024.
- Rythu Bandhu began 2018 (BRS) at `₹4,000`/acre/season initially, later `₹5,000`.
- PM-KISAN comparison: `₹6,000`/family/year Central scheme — can co-exist.
- Related: Rythu Bima (life cover for farmers) continues — *verify current terms*.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **The Ikshvakus and the Vishnukundins**

Between the Satavahanas and the Chalukyas, two dynasties kept the Telugu country's lamps burning — one Buddhist-brilliant, one Vedic-vigorous.

**The Ikshvakus (c. 3rd century CE, ~100 years):**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Capital | **Vijayapuri** (Nagarjunakonda, on the Krishna — the *Sriparvata* region) |
| Founder | **Vasishthiputra Chamtamula** — performed *asvamedha* and *vajapeya* sacrifices |
| Peak | **Virapurushadatta** — the "Buddhist age" of the dynasty |
| Later rulers | Ehuvala Chamtamula, Rudrapurushadatta |

- **Royal women built Buddhism**: **Chamtisiri** (founder's sister) raised the great **Mahachaitya** at Nagarjunakonda; other queens endowed viharas for monks from Sri Lanka, Kashmir, Gandhara, even China — making it an **international Buddhist university-town** linked to Acharya **Nagarjuna**.
- Pattern to remember: **kings performed Vedic sacrifices while their queens patronised Buddhism** — a textbook case of coexistence.
- Art: the **Nagarjunakonda school** of limestone sculpture continued Amaravati traditions; monuments were relocated to a hilltop museum when **Nagarjunasagar dam** submerged the valley (1960s).

**The Vishnukundins (c. 5th–7th century CE):**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Core area | Telangana–coastal Andhra; capitals linked to **Amarapura/Indrapalanagara** (Nalgonda region) and sites like **Keesaragutta** (near Hyderabad) |
| Greatest ruler | **Madhava Varma II** — inscriptions credit him with `11` *asvamedhas* and `1,000` *agnistomas*; title *Trivaranagara-bhavanagata-sundari-hridaya-nandana* (verify exact reading) |
| Religion | Mostly Vedic-Shaivite; **Govinda Varma** and **Vikramendra Varma** patronised Buddhism too |
| Legacy | Rock-cut architecture (e.g., **Undavalli caves** attributed); lion-motif coins; matrimonial ties with the **Vakatakas** |
| End | Power eclipsed by the rising **Chalukyas of Badami** (Pulakeshin II's eastern campaigns, early 7th century) |

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Keesaragutta** (Medchal–Malkajgiri, near Hyderabad) is the Vishnukundin site closest to the capital city — excavated temples, bricks and inscriptions make it TGPSC's favourite "near-Hyderabad archaeology" question.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**I**kshvaku = **I**nternational Buddhism (Nagarjunakonda); **V**ishnukundin = **V**edic sacrifices (Madhava Varma II's 11 asvamedhas)."

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Nagarjunakonda is on the **Krishna**, not the Godavari; and the Ikshvakus of the south are **not** the epic Suryavamshi Ikshvakus of Ayodhya — examiners exploit both confusions.

**💡 Example:** When Nagarjunasagar's waters rose, India executed one of the world's largest archaeology rescues — dismantling and rebuilding the Mahachaitya and viharas brick-by-brick on **Nagarjunakonda island**. One dam question, one culture question, one history question — same coordinates.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Ikshvaku capital Vijayapuri; founder Chamtamula; Buddhist patron queens (Chamtisiri).
- Virapurushadatta's era = peak of Nagarjunakonda Buddhism.
- Vishnukundin greatest: Madhava Varma II; sites: Keesaragutta, Indrapalanagara.
- Both dynasties bridge Satavahanas → (Badami) Chalukyas.
- Nagarjunakonda sculpture = limestone, Amaravati-school lineage.
- Vishnukundin coins: lion symbol; Vakataka marriage alliance.

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **The Birth of the INC & the Moderate Era (1885–1905)**

Yesterday's associations converge into one all-India platform — and its first twenty years define the "Moderate" method.

**The founding (1885):**

- `28 December 1885` — first session at **Gokuldas Tejpal Sanskrit College, Bombay**; `72` delegates.
- First president: **W.C. Bonnerjee**; chief organiser: retired ICS officer **A.O. Hume** (the "father of the INC").
- Venue shifted from Poona due to a plague outbreak.
- Theories of origin: Hume's "**safety valve**" (channel discontent safely) vs the nationalist "**lightning conductor**" reply (leaders *used* Hume as cover) — Lala Lajpat Rai popularised the critique.

**Presidents worth memorising:**

| Year | President | First… |
|---|---|---|
| 1885 | W.C. Bonnerjee | first president |
| 1886 | Dadabhai Naoroji | (also 1893, 1906) |
| 1887 | **Badruddin Tyabji** | first Muslim president |
| 1888 | **George Yule** | first English president |
| 1905 | Gokhale | presided at Banaras during Swadeshi storm |

**The Moderate method — the "3 Ps":** **P**etitions, **P**rayers, **P**rotests (within law) — resolutions, articles, delegations to Britain; deep faith in British justice.

**Demands:** Indianisation of civil services (simultaneous exams), expansion of councils, separation of judiciary from executive, cut in military spending, end of the drain.

**Contributions:**
1. **Economic critique** — Naoroji's **Drain Theory** (*Poverty and Un-British Rule in India*), R.C. Dutt's *Economic History of India* — the movement's intellectual spine.
2. **Indian Councils Act `1892`** — indirect elections, wider discussion powers (their concrete legislative win).
3. Built an all-India political community and trained the next generation.

**Limitations:** narrow social base ("microscopic minority" jibe by Viceroy **Dufferin**), no mass contact, results meagre — the ground the Extremists would seize after `1905`.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The INC's first session was in **Bombay (1885)** under **Bonnerjee** — but the *organisation* idea matured through Hume's correspondence and the 1884 Theosophical convention circles.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — First trio: "**B**ombay, **B**onnerjee, **B**ritish-officer Hume" — the three B's of 1885.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — First **Muslim** president = Badruddin **Tyabji** (1887), first **English** = George **Yule** (1888) — the pair gets swapped constantly. Also 1885 ≠ Calcutta; the *second* session (1886) was Calcutta.

**💡 Example:** From **Hyderabad State**, early Congress sympathy stayed muted — the Nizam's government barred open political activity, so nationalist energy flowed through the **library movement and press** instead (recall Day 2's Sri Krishna Devaraya Andhra Bhasha Nilayam link in History) — a classic TGPSC cross-subject connection.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- 72 delegates; surgeon-education mix; no Muslim League yet (1906 later).
- Naoroji: first Indian in the **British House of Commons** (1892, Liberal).
- "Un-British" framing = appeal to Britain's own ideals.
- Gokhale founded **Servants of India Society** (`1905`).
- Dufferin was Viceroy at the founding; called INC a "microscopic minority".
- The Moderates' economic nationalism outlived their politics — reused by every later phase.

## 📘 Subject 5: Telangana Geography (with Maps)

### Let's learn about **The Krishna System in Depth**

![The Godavari and Krishna in Telangana — entry points, confluences, projects and bank temples (schematic, not to scale)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/faizan574/Learning-Python/main/tgpsc-prep/maps/telangana_rivers.svg)
*🗺️ Today follow the thick blue southern line — the Krishna from Tangadi to Nagarjunasagar.*

Yesterday the Godavari; today its southern twin, the river of dams, disputes and Buddhist valleys.

**The river:**
- Rises at **Mahabaleshwar** (Satara, Maharashtra); length about `1,400 km` — India's **fourth-longest** river system (after Ganga, Godavari, Yamuna-counted systems — *verify ranking convention*).
- Receives the **Bhima** (north) and the mighty **Tungabhadra** (south) *before/at* the Telangana stretch; enters Telangana at **Tangadi** (Jogulamba Gadwal district).
- Flows east along the state's southern edge — through the **Nallamala gorge** — and exits below Nagarjunasagar towards the AP delta (Vijayawada's Prakasam barrage).

**Tributaries relevant to Telangana:**

| Tributary | Joins | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Tungabhadra** | Near **Alampur** (Sangameswaram) | Largest tributary; Jogulamba temple at the confluence |
| **Bhima** | Upstream (Karnataka) | Northern arm draining Maharashtra-Karnataka |
| **Dindi** | Mid-course | Has its own reservoir + LIS |
| **Musi** | **Vadapally** (Nalgonda) | Flows through Hyderabad; twin lakes upstream |
| **Halia, Paleru, Munneru** | Lower course | Munneru is the last major one in the Telugu states |

**Projects west → east:**

1. **Priyadarshini Jurala** — first Telangana project on the Krishna; hydel + irrigation (with Lower Jurala hydel).
2. **Srisailam** — huge reservoir in the Nallamala (dam in AP; **left-bank hydel station is Telangana's**); feeds **Kalwakurthy LIS** and the **SLBC tunnel** (Srisailam Left Bank Canal — Asia's longest irrigation tunnel project, under construction, site of the 2025 tunnel-collapse incident — *verify status*).
3. **Nagarjunasagar** — at Nandikonda; among the world's largest **masonry dams**; left canal (**Lal Bahadur Shastri canal**) irrigates Nalgonda-Suryapet-Khammam.
4. **Pulichintala** — balancing reservoir on the AP border.
5. **Palamuru–Rangareddy LIS** — the under-construction mega-lift for the dry south (*verify commissioning status*).

**Water-sharing (the dispute layer):**
- **KWDT-I (Bachawat Tribunal)** allocated Krishna waters among Maharashtra-Karnataka-AP; **KWDT-II (Brijesh Kumar)** award is contested; post-2014, AP vs Telangana sharing has run on ad-hoc arrangements (commonly `66:34` — *verify latest*), managed by the **Krishna River Management Board (KRMB)**.
- The **Musi's 1908 flood** killed thousands and led the Nizam (advised by engineer **M. Visvesvaraya**) to build **Osmansagar (1920)** and **Himayatsagar (1927)** — flood control + drinking water; today's **Musi Rejuvenation** project is the current-affairs sequel.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The **Tungabhadra joins the Krishna near Alampur**, and the **Jogulamba Shakti Peetha** stands at that confluence — geography and culture in one dot (remember the Mahabubnagar map!).

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Project order west→east: "**J**ust **S**ee **N**agarjuna's **P**alace" — **J**urala, **S**risailam, **N**agarjunasagar, **P**ulichintala.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Srisailam **dam** lies in AP, but its **left-bank powerhouse belongs to Telangana**; Nagarjunasagar's **right** canal (Jawahar) serves AP while the **left** (Lal Bahadur) serves Telangana. Left/right swaps are the exam's favourite trick here.

**💡 Example:** In drought years, releases from Srisailam vs Nagarjunasagar become nightly news — the KRMB's gate-control disputes of recent years are a ready-made "geography + polity + current affairs" three-in-one question.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Origin Mahabaleshwar; entry Tangadi; largest tributary Tungabhadra.
- Musi-Krishna confluence: Vadapally; Musi's twin lakes: Osmansagar + Himayatsagar.
- Nagarjunasagar = Nandikonda site, masonry giant, 1955–67 construction, left canal = Lal Bahadur.
- Kalwakurthy, Nettempadu, Bhima, Koilsagar = Palamuru-region lift schemes (Krishna basin).
- Krishna Pushkaralu every `12` years (last `2016`).
- KWDT-I = Bachawat; KWDT-II = Brijesh Kumar; board = KRMB.
