## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **Fundamental Rights: Articles 15–18 (Right to Equality contd.)**

After Article 14's general equality, Articles 15–18 spell out **specific equalities** — the constitutional home of India's reservation policy.

**Article 15 — Prohibition of discrimination:**
- `15(1)`: State shall not discriminate against any **citizen** on grounds *only* of **religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth** (the "**RRCSP**" grounds).
- `15(2)`: No discrimination in access to shops, public places, wells, roads.
- `15(3)`: State **can make special provisions for women and children**.
- `15(4)` (1st Amendment, `1951`): special provisions for **socially and educationally backward classes (SEBCs), SCs & STs**.
- `15(5)` (93rd Amendment, `2005`): reservation in **educational institutions** including private (except minority) — basis of OBC quota in colleges.
- `15(6)` (103rd Amendment, `2019`): up to **10% for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS)**.

**Article 16 — Equality of opportunity in public employment:**
- `16(1)-(2)`: equal opportunity; no discrimination in State jobs.
- `16(3)`: Parliament may prescribe **residence** requirement for certain jobs.
- `16(4)`: reservation for **backward classes** inadequately represented.
- `16(4A)`: reservation in **promotions** for SC/ST; `16(4B)`: carry-forward of unfilled vacancies.
- `16(6)`: **EWS** reservation in jobs (103rd Amendment).
- **Indra Sawhney (1992)**: upheld OBC quota, capped total reservation at **50%**, introduced the **"creamy layer"** exclusion.

**Article 17 — Abolition of untouchability:** untouchability abolished; its practice an offence (**Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955**).

**Article 18 — Abolition of titles:** no titles except **military and academic**; citizens can't accept foreign titles. (Bharat Ratna/Padma awards are **not** titles — upheld in *Balaji Raghavan* case.)

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Indra Sawhney (1992)** = the reservation bible: 50% ceiling, creamy layer, no reservation in promotions (later restored for SC/ST via `16(4A)`).

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Article 15 grounds "**RRCSP**": Religion, Race, Caste, Sex, Place of birth. EWS = **10%**, added by the **103rd** Amendment.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Art 15 protects **citizens only**; Art 15(1) says "*only*" — discrimination on these grounds *plus others* may be valid. Bharat Ratna is **not** a title under Art 18.

**💡 Example:** Telangana's move toward **42% BC reservation** (from the caste survey, recall Day 2/upcoming) runs straight into the **Indra Sawhney 50% ceiling** — the exact Article 16 tension examiners love to test.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- 15(3) women & children; 15(4) SEBC/SC/ST (1st Amd); 15(5) education (93rd); 15(6) EWS (103rd).
- 16(4A) SC/ST promotions; 16(4B) carry-forward; creamy layer applies to OBC (not SC/ST for now).
- 50% ceiling from Indra Sawhney; EWS 10% is *over and above*, upheld in *Janhit Abhiyan (2022)*.
- Untouchability = Art 17; PCR Act 1955 + SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989.
- Titles: only military/academic allowed.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **The Kakatiyas — Administration, Economy, Society & Architecture**

Day 5 gave the kings; today the civilisation — the *nayankara* system, the tanks, and the temples that made Warangal a UNESCO name.

**Administration — the Nayankara system:**
- Land/military organised under **nayakas** (military chiefs) granted **nayankaramu** (assignments of land/revenue) in return for maintaining troops — a **feudal-military** structure later inherited by Vijayanagara.
- **Angaraksha / Ayagar** village functionaries; strong fort-based defence (Warangal's triple walls).
- Officers: *bahattara-niyogadhipati* (72 officers tradition). Kingdom divided into **nadus/sthalas**.

**Economy — water is wealth:**
- Massive **tank (cheruvu) irrigation** — chain tanks like **Pakhal, Laknavaram, Ramappa (Bayyaram), Ghanpur** — the backbone of agriculture (the inspiration for modern **Mission Kakatiya**).
- Flourishing **textile & diamond trade**; the port of **Motupalli** (Ganapati Deva's charter) linked to overseas commerce; guilds and fairs.

**Society & religion:**
- **Shaivism** dominant (Pashupata, Kalamukha sects); also Jainism and Veerashaivism; temple-centred social life.
- Notable liberalism: women's participation (Rudrama Devi herself); the *Motupalli* trade welcome to foreigners.

**Architecture — the crown jewels:**

| Monument | Note |
|---|---|
| **Thousand Pillar Temple** (Hanamkonda) | Trikutalaya (3 shrines — Shiva, Vishnu, Surya), star-shaped, built by Rudradeva `1163` |
| **Ramappa (Rudreshwara) Temple**, Palampet | Built by **Recharla Rukmangada** (Ganapati Deva's general), c. `1213`; **floating bricks**, Nandi, bracket figures; **UNESCO World Heritage `2021`** |
| **Warangal Fort & Kala Thoranam** | The gateway on the **Telangana state emblem** |
| **Kakatiya sculpture** | Dolerite/black basalt, high polish, *madanikas* (dancing figures) |

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Ramappa Temple (Palampet)** is a **UNESCO World Heritage Site (2021)** — India's nomination that year — famous for its **lightweight "floating" bricks** and the sculptor **Ramappa** (the only temple named after its artisan).

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Two temples: "**Thousand Pillars at Hanamkonda (1163), Ramappa at Palampet (UNESCO 2021)**" — Rudradeva vs Recharla Rukmangada.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Ramappa is at **Palampet (Mulugu)**, built by **Recharla Rukmangada** under Ganapati Deva — not by the king directly. The Thousand Pillar Temple is the one by Rudradeva (1163).

**💡 Example:** **Mission Kakatiya** (restoration of ~46,000 minor irrigation tanks) is named after this dynasty's tank network — a direct line from 13th-century *cheruvus* to 21st-century policy, the classic history-to-governance question.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Nayankara system → inherited by Vijayanagara.
- Tank irrigation: Pakhal, Laknavaram, Ramappa, Ghanpur; Mission Kakatiya legacy.
- Thousand Pillar Temple — Rudradeva 1163, trikutalaya.
- Ramappa — Recharla Rukmangada ~1213, UNESCO 2021, floating bricks.
- Motupalli port + textile/diamond trade.
- Religion: Shaivism dominant; Rudrama Devi's era = women's prominence.

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **Morley-Minto & Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms, Lucknow Pact & Home Rule**

The 1909–1919 decade: constitutional reforms, a rare Hindu-Muslim pact, and the Home Rule agitation that revived nationalism.

**Morley-Minto Reforms (Indian Councils Act, `1909`):**
- Expanded legislative councils; introduced **indirect elections**.
- **Separate electorates for Muslims** — the seed of communal politics (Minto called the "father of communal electorate").
- No real power transfer; officials still dominated.

**Lucknow Pact (`1916`):**
- At Lucknow, the **Congress and Muslim League** signed a pact — the high point of **Hindu-Muslim unity**.
- Congress accepted **separate electorates**; both demanded self-government.
- Also united **Moderates and Extremists** back into one Congress (Tilak readmitted).

**Home Rule Movement (`1916`):**
- Two leagues: **Tilak's Home Rule League** (April 1916, Maharashtra/Karnataka/CP) and **Annie Besant's** (Sept 1916, rest of India, via her paper *New India*).
- Demand: **self-government (Home Rule) within the Empire**, inspired by the Irish model.
- Besant was interned (1917), sparking wide protest; she became **INC President (1917)** — the first woman.

**Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (Government of India Act, `1919`):**
- Preceded by the **August Declaration (1917)** — Secretary of State **Montagu** promised "gradual development of self-governing institutions".
- Introduced **Dyarchy** in the provinces: subjects split into **"Reserved"** (governor + executive council) and **"Transferred"** (ministers responsible to legislature).
- **Bicameral** central legislature (Council of State + Legislative Assembly); direct elections widened; a **statutory commission after 10 years** (→ Simon Commission, 1927).

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Dyarchy (double government) in the provinces** is the defining feature of the **Montagu-Chelmsford (1919)** reforms; **separate electorates** are the defining feature of **Morley-Minto (1909)**.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**Minto → Muslim electorates (1909); Montford → Dyarchy (1919); Lucknow → unity (1916).**"

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — 1909 = Morley-**Minto** (separate electorates); 1919 = Montagu-**Chelmsford** (dyarchy). Don't swap. Lucknow Pact (1916) is where Congress *accepted* separate electorates.

**💡 Example:** **Annie Besant's** Home Rule agitation, run partly through the **Theosophical Society HQ at Adyar**, shows the movement's revival between the two Gandhi-less lulls — a bridge from the Extremists to the Gandhian era.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- 1909 Morley-Minto: separate electorates, indirect elections.
- 1916 Lucknow Pact: Congress-League unity + Moderate-Extremist reunion.
- 1916 Home Rule: Tilak (April) & Besant (Sept); "Swaraj is my birthright" energised it.
- 1917 August Declaration by Montagu; Besant first woman INC President.
- 1919 Montford: dyarchy (reserved/transferred), bicameral centre, 10-year review.

## 📘 Subject 5: Telangana Geography (with Maps)

### Let's learn about **Irrigation Projects — Major, Medium & Lift Schemes**

![The Godavari and Krishna in Telangana — projects and confluences (schematic, not to scale)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/faizan574/Learning-Python/main/tgpsc-prep/maps/telangana_rivers.svg)
*🗺️ Trace both rivers on the map: Godavari projects along the north, Krishna projects along the south.*

Telangana's "lift-irrigation" identity — how a plateau with rivers in deep valleys waters its fields.

**Why lifts?** The Godavari and Krishna flow in **deep gorges** far below the cultivable plateau, so water must be **pumped up (lifted)** rather than flowing by gravity — hence Telangana's giant **Lift Irrigation Schemes (LIS)**.

**Godavari-basin projects:**

| Project | Note |
|---|---|
| **Sriram Sagar (SRSP)**, Pochampadu | "Lifeline of north Telangana" |
| **Nizamsagar** (on Manjira) | Nizam-era, oldest major |
| **Kaleshwaram LIS** | World's largest multi-stage lift; Medigadda-Annaram-Sundilla barrages at the Pranahita confluence (*verify current status after barrage issues*) |
| **Sripada Yellampalli**, Devadula, Sitarama LIS | Newer eastern lifts |
| **Kadem, Sri Komaram Bheem** | Adilabad-region projects |

**Krishna-basin projects:**

| Project | Note |
|---|---|
| **Nagarjunasagar** | Masonry giant; Lal Bahadur (left) canal serves Telangana |
| **Srisailam** | Left-bank powerhouse is Telangana's; feeds Kalwakurthy LIS & SLBC tunnel |
| **Priyadarshini Jurala** | First Telangana project on the Krishna |
| **Palamuru–Rangareddy LIS** | Mega-lift for the dry south (under construction — *verify commissioning*) |
| **Kalwakurthy, Nettempadu, Bhima, Koilsagar** | Palamuru lift schemes |

**Classification:** **Major** (>10,000 ha ayacut), **Medium** (2,000–10,000 ha), **Minor** (<2,000 ha — the tank sector, revived by **Mission Kakatiya**). Drinking water: **Mission Bhagiratha** (piped water to every home).

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Kaleshwaram** is the world's largest multi-stage **lift** irrigation project (Godavari, at the Pranahita confluence); **Palamuru–Rangareddy** is the big Krishna-basin lift for the drought-prone south.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**Godavari north, Krishna south**": SRSP/Kaleshwaram (Godavari) vs Nagarjunasagar/Jurala/Palamuru-Rangareddy (Krishna).

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — **Mission Kakatiya** = minor **tank** restoration; **Mission Bhagiratha** = **drinking**-water pipelines — don't mix them. Kaleshwaram is on the **Godavari**, not the Krishna.

**💡 Example:** Kaleshwaram lifts Godavari water **hundreds of metres** up through a chain of pump-houses and reservoirs (Medigadda → Annaram → Sundilla → Yellampalli → Mid Manair…) — engineering that answers "why does Telangana need lifts?" in one project.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Kaleshwaram = world's largest lift, Godavari, Pranahita confluence.
- SRSP (Pochampadu) = north Telangana lifeline; Nizamsagar on Manjira.
- Nagarjunasagar left canal (Lal Bahadur) serves Telangana; Jurala = first on Krishna.
- Palamuru-Rangareddy LIS = dry-south mega-lift.
- Mission Kakatiya = tanks; Mission Bhagiratha = drinking water.
- Ayacut sizes: Major >10,000 ha, Medium 2,000–10,000, Minor <2,000.
