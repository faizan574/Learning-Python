## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **Fundamental Rights: Articles 19–22 (Right to Freedom)**

The freedom cluster — the most litigated Fundamental Rights, covering expression, life, and protection against arrest.

**Article 19 — Six freedoms (citizens only):**

| Sub-clause | Freedom |
|---|---|
| `19(1)(a)` | Speech & expression (includes press, RTI, right to silence) |
| `19(1)(b)` | Assemble peaceably, **without arms** |
| `19(1)(c)` | Form associations / unions / cooperatives (97th Amd) |
| `19(1)(d)` | Move freely throughout India |
| `19(1)(e)` | Reside & settle in any part |
| `19(1)(g)` | Practise any profession / trade |

(The old `19(1)(f)` right to property was **deleted by the 44th Amendment, 1978** → now a legal right under `Art 300A`.) Each freedom has **"reasonable restrictions"** (security, public order, decency, sovereignty, etc.).

**Article 20 — Protection in respect of conviction:**
- No **ex-post-facto** law (no retrospective criminal punishment);
- No **double jeopardy** (no punishment twice for the same offence);
- No **self-incrimination** (`20(3)` — accused can't be compelled to be a witness against himself).

**Article 21 — Protection of life & personal liberty:** "No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to **procedure established by law**." Post **Maneka Gandhi (1978)**, the procedure must be **fair, just and reasonable** — Article 21 now includes rights to privacy (**Puttaswamy, 2017**), clean environment, livelihood, speedy trial, dignity, etc.
- **Article 21A** (86th Amendment, 2002): **free & compulsory education for 6–14 years**.

**Article 22 — Protection against arrest & detention:** rights on arrest (informed of grounds, lawyer, produced before magistrate within **24 hours**) — but these don't apply to **enemy aliens** or **preventive detention**. Preventive detention max **3 months** without advisory board review.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Article 21 (life & personal liberty)** and **Article 20** are the only Fundamental Rights that **cannot be suspended even during a National Emergency** (44th Amendment).

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Art 19 six freedoms "**SAAM-RP**": Speech, Assembly, Association, Movement, Residence, Profession. Art 20 = "no **retro, no repeat, no self-tell**".

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Art 19 = **citizens only**; Art 20, 21, 22 = **all persons**. Right to property is **no longer** a Fundamental Right (44th Amd) — it's `Art 300A`.

**💡 Example:** The **right to privacy** (Puttaswamy, 2017) and the decriminalisation rulings all flow from **Article 21's** expanding scope — the single most powerful Fundamental Right, and a certain TGPSC question.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- 19(1)(f) property deleted by 44th Amd → 300A.
- 20 & 21 non-suspendable even in emergency (44th Amd).
- Maneka Gandhi (1978): due-process reading of Art 21.
- Art 21A: 6–14 yrs education (86th Amd).
- Art 22: 24-hour production; preventive detention safeguards.
- Reasonable restrictions differ per freedom (e.g., 19(2) for speech).

## 📘 Subject 2: Telangana Schemes & Important Incidents

### Let's learn about **The Caste Survey (SEEEPC) & the 42% BC Reservation Issue**

The biggest political-administrative "incident" of the current government — a full caste census and the quota push that followed.

**The survey:**
- The **Socio, Economic, Educational, Employment, Political and Caste (SEEEPC) Survey** — a door-to-door enumeration begun **November 2024**; findings released early **2025**.
- Headline finding: **Backward Classes ≈ 56%** of the population (including BC Muslims); SC, ST, OC shares also mapped (*verify exact figures from official release*).
- Positioned as the first big state caste survey, a model for other states.

**The 42% BC reservation move:**
- Using the survey data, the Assembly passed bills to raise **BC reservation to 42%** in **education, employment and local bodies**.
- Combined with existing SC/ST/EWS quotas, the total **crosses the 50% ceiling** (Indra Sawhney) — so the bills were sent for **central/Presidential assent** and Ninth-Schedule protection was sought; local-body reservation was pursued through ordinance/GO routes — a live legal-political issue (*verify current status*).
- Panchayat elections became the flashpoint for applying the 42% BC quota in local bodies.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The **SEEEPC caste survey (Nov 2024)** found BCs ~**56%**, driving the **42% BC reservation** push that collides with the **50% Indra Sawhney ceiling**.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**Survey → 56% BC → 42% quota → 50% ceiling clash**" — the chain of the whole issue.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — SEEEPC = the **caste survey** (2024–25); don't confuse with the older Samagra Kutumba Survey (2014) or Praja Palana (2023). BC quota target = **42%**.

**💡 Example:** When the government tried to apply **42% BC reservation in panchayat elections**, it ran straight into the **50% ceiling** and courts — the exact geography-of-quota problem tested as "caste survey → reservation → constitutional limit."

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**

- 42% BC quota in education, jobs, local bodies.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **Musunuri Nayaks, Recherla Velamas & the Bahmani Kingdom**

The turbulent 14th–15th centuries: after Warangal fell (1323), Telugu warriors reclaimed it, then the Deccan split into new sultanates.

**Musunuri Nayaks (c. 1323–1368):**
- After the Kakatiya collapse, **Musunuri Prolaya Nayaka** and his cousin **Musunuri Kapaya Nayaka** led a confederacy of ~75 *nayakas* to **expel the Delhi Sultanate** and **recapture Warangal (c. 1336)**.
- The **Vilasa grant** of Prolaya Nayaka records the resistance and restoration of order.
- Kapaya Nayaka's power waned against the Bahmanis and the Recherla Velamas; he died c. 1368.

**Recherla Velamas (Rachakonda & Devarakonda):**
- A Velama chieftain line (formerly Kakatiya/Musunuri subordinates) that rose to rule from **Rachakonda and Devarakonda** (Nalgonda region).
- **Anapota Nayaka** and **Singama Nayaka** were prominent; they clashed with Kapaya Nayaka and the Reddy kingdom, and allied/contended with the Bahmanis.

**Bahmani Kingdom (1347–c. 1518):**
- Founded by **Alauddin Hasan Bahman Shah (Hasan Gangu)** in **1347**, breaking from the Delhi Sultanate; capital first **Gulbarga (Ahsanabad)**, later **Bidar (Muhammadabad)**.
- Greatest phase under the minister **Mahmud Gawan** (Persian statesman; administrative & educational reforms, the **Madrasa at Bidar**); executed 1481, after which the kingdom declined.
- Bahmani-Vijayanagara rivalry over the **Raichur Doab** (Krishna-Tungabhadra) shaped the Deccan; the kingdom later fragmented into the **five Deccan Sultanates** — including **Golconda (Qutb Shahi)**, tomorrow's story.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Musunuri Kapaya Nayaka recaptured Warangal (~1336)** from the Delhi Sultanate — a Telugu reconquest; the **Bahmani kingdom (1347)** then rose as the Deccan's first independent Muslim sultanate.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Sequence: "**Kakatiya fall (1323) → Musunuri reconquest (1336) → Bahmani rise (1347) → Golconda later.**"

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — **Musunuri** (Prolaya & Kapaya) reconquered Warangal; **Recherla Velamas** ruled Rachakonda/Devarakonda — different lines. Bahmani founder = **Hasan Gangu (Bahman Shah)**, capital Gulbarga → **Bidar**.

**💡 Example:** The **Recherla Velamas of Rachakonda-Devarakonda** built hill forts still standing in Nalgonda — a bridge dynasty between the Kakatiyas and the Qutb Shahis, and a favourite "who ruled Telangana between 1323 and 1518?" question.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Musunuri Prolaya (Vilasa grant) & Kapaya Nayaka; Warangal recaptured ~1336.
- Recherla Velamas: Rachakonda & Devarakonda (Nalgonda).
- Bahmani founded 1347 by Hasan Gangu; capitals Gulbarga → Bidar.
- Mahmud Gawan: reforms, Bidar madrasa; executed 1481.
- Bahmani split → 5 Deccan Sultanates (incl. Golconda/Qutb Shahi).

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **Gandhi's Arrival & Early Satyagrahas (Champaran, Kheda, Ahmedabad)**

Gandhi returns from South Africa and tests **satyagraha** on Indian soil — three experiments that launched the Gandhian era.

**Return & apprenticeship:**
- Gandhi reached India on **`9 January 1915`** (now **Pravasi Bharatiya Divas**); on **Gokhale's** advice he toured India for a year; founded the **Sabarmati (Satyagraha) Ashram** near Ahmedabad.

**Champaran Satyagraha (`1917`) — first civil disobedience in India:**
- Against the **tinkathia system** forcing indigo cultivation (3/20th of land) on Bihar's peasants under European planters.
- Gandhi defied orders to leave; a committee's inquiry ended the planters' exploitation. Assisted by **Rajendra Prasad, J.B. Kripalani, Brajkishore Prasad**.

**Ahmedabad Mill Strike (`1918`) — first hunger strike:**
- A dispute between mill workers and owners over the **plague bonus / wages**; Gandhi advised workers to strike and undertook a **fast**; workers won a **35% wage hike**.

**Kheda Satyagraha (`1918`) — first non-cooperation:**
- Gujarat peasants, hit by **crop failure**, sought suspension of land revenue (allowed under law if yield < 25%); the government refused. Gandhi led a **no-tax campaign**; **Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel** rose here as a leader.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Champaran (1917)** = Gandhi's **first satyagraha/civil disobedience in India**; **Ahmedabad (1918)** = first **hunger strike**; **Kheda (1918)** = first **non-cooperation** (no-tax).

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**C-A-K**": **C**hamparan (indigo, 1917), **A**hmedabad (mill, fast, 1918), **K**heda (revenue, Patel, 1918).

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Champaran = **indigo/Bihar** (tinkathia); Kheda = **revenue/Gujarat** (Patel emerges). Don't swap. Gandhi returned in **1915**, first satyagraha in **1917**.

**💡 Example:** At **Champaran**, Gandhi turned a local indigo grievance into a moral-legal confrontation the Raj couldn't handle — the template (local issue → mass mobilisation → negotiated win) he'd scale up to national movements.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Return 9 Jan 1915; Sabarmati Ashram; Gokhale = political guru.
- Champaran 1917 — tinkathia indigo; Rajendra Prasad assisted.
- Ahmedabad 1918 — mill strike, Gandhi's first fast, 35% hike, Anasuya Sarabhai.
- Kheda 1918 — no-tax; Patel emerges.
- These built Gandhi's mass base before Non-Cooperation (1920).

### Let's learn about **Forests, Wildlife Sanctuaries & Tiger Reserves**

![Schematic map of Telangana — regions, forests and cultural landmarks](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/faizan574/Learning-Python/main/tgpsc-prep/maps/telangana_regions.svg)
*🗺️ Locate the forest belts: Kawal (north/Adilabad), Amrabad (south/Nallamala), Eturnagaram-Medaram (east/Mulugu).*

The green geography — where the tigers, the Chenchus and the Gonds live.

**Forest cover:** roughly **`24%`** of the state's area (*verify latest ISFR figure*), concentrated in **Adilabad (north), the Nallamala (south) and Bhadradri-Mulugu (east)**.

**Tiger Reserves (two):**

| Reserve | Location | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Amrabad** | Nallamala hills (Nagarkurnool) | Among India's **largest**; **Chenchu** tribal homeland; uranium-exploration controversy |
| **Kawal** | Adilabad/Kumram Bheem | Gond country; Godavari-side forests |

**Wildlife Sanctuaries / Parks:**
- **Eturnagaram** (Mulugu) — one of the **oldest** sanctuaries; hosts **Medaram** (Sammakka-Saralamma Jatara).
- **Pakhal** (around the Kakatiya-built lake), **Kinnerasani** (Khammam), **Pocharam, Manjira** (crocodiles/birds), **Sivaram, Pranihita, Kawal**.
- In Hyderabad: **KBR National Park**, **Mrugavani**, **Mahavir Harina Vanasthali** (blackbuck).

**Waterfalls & green spots:** **Kuntala** (highest in Telangana, Adilabad), **Bogatha** ("Telangana's Niagara", Mulugu), **Mallela Theertham** (Nallamala).

**Tribal-forest link:** **Chenchus** (Nallamala), **Gonds & Kolams** (Adilabad), **Koyas** (Bhadradri-Mulugu) — forests are both ecology and living culture (Medaram, Nagoba Jatara).

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — Telangana's **two tiger reserves are Amrabad (Nallamala) and Kawal (Adilabad)**; Amrabad is among India's largest and is the **Chenchu** homeland.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Tigers "**A-K**": **A**mrabad (south, Nallamala) & **K**awal (north, Adilabad). Highest waterfall = **Kuntala**.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Only **two** tiger reserves (Amrabad, Kawal). Eturnagaram/Pakhal/Kinnerasani are **sanctuaries**, not tiger reserves. KBR is a **national park** in Hyderabad.

**💡 Example:** **Medaram** ties it together: a **sanctuary** (Eturnagaram), a **river** (Jampanna Vagu), and Asia's largest **tribal jatara** (Sammakka-Saralamma) — one forest dot answering geography, culture and history at once.
