## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **Fundamental Rights: Articles 12–14**

Part III (`Articles 12–35`) is the heart of the Constitution. Today we open it: the definition of the "State", the safety of existing laws, and the bedrock **Right to Equality**.

**Article 12 — Definition of "State":**
For the purposes of Part III, **"State"** includes:
1. The **Government and Parliament of India** (Union executive + legislature)
2. The **Government and Legislature of each State**
3. **All local authorities** (municipalities, panchayats, district boards)
4. **Other authorities** within India or under the Government's control

- The phrase **"other authorities"** has been widely interpreted — bodies like LIC, ONGC, and even some private bodies performing **public functions** have been held to be "State" (so Fundamental Rights can be enforced against them).

**Article 13 — Laws inconsistent with Fundamental Rights:**
- `13(1)`: **Pre-Constitution** laws inconsistent with FRs are **void** (to the extent of inconsistency) — the **Doctrine of Eclipse**.
- `13(2)`: The State shall **not make** any law taking away FRs; such laws are void — the **Doctrine of Severability**.
- `13(4)` (24th Amendment): a **constitutional amendment** under `Art 368` is not a "law" under Article 13 — but **Kesavananda Bharati** subjected amendments to the **basic structure** test anyway.
- Article 13 is the textual basis of **judicial review**.

**Article 14 — Equality before Law:**
- Guarantees to **every person** (citizens *and* foreigners): **"equality before the law"** (British, Dicey — no one above the law) and **"equal protection of the laws"** (American — equals treated equally).
- Permits **reasonable classification** (intelligible differentia + rational nexus with the objective) but forbids **class legislation**.
- **E.P. Royappa (1974)** — arbitrariness is the antithesis of equality; Article 14 embodies a guarantee against **arbitrary State action**.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Article 14 protects "every person"**, not just citizens — foreigners on Indian soil are covered. (Contrast: Art 15, 16, 19 are citizen-only.)

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Two halves of Art 14: "**B**efore = **B**ritish (rule of law); **P**rotection = the **P**ond (US — like fish treated alike)."

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — "Equality **before** law" (negative, British) vs "equal **protection** of laws" (positive, American) — examiners swap their origins. Also Doctrine of **Eclipse** = pre-Constitution laws; **Severability** = striking the bad part only.

**💡 Example:** Reserving bus seats for women, or separate income-tax slabs, are valid **reasonable classifications** under Art 14. But "no person of surname X may hold a government job" would be arbitrary **class legislation** — void. A ready application MCQ.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Art 12 "State" includes local + "other authorities"; test = deep governmental control / public function.
- Doctrine of Eclipse (13(1)) vs Severability (13(2)).
- Art 13 = basis of judicial review.
- Art 14: every person; reasonable classification allowed, class legislation barred.
- Royappa (1974) = arbitrariness doctrine; also relevant: *Air India v. Nargesh Meerza*.
- "Rule of law" (Dicey) has 3 elements: supremacy of law, equality before law, predominance of legal spirit.

## 📘 Subject 2: Telangana Schemes & Important Incidents

### Let's learn about **Cheyutha Pensions & Rajiv Aarogyasri**

Social security for the old, disabled and sick — the safety-net guarantee. Its pension amounts and the health-cover ceiling are prime exam figures.

**Cheyutha (Aasara) pensions:**

| Category | Promised monthly pension |
|---|---|
| Old age, widows, single women, weavers, toddy tappers, beedi workers, HIV+, etc. | **`₹4,000`** |
| **Persons with disabilities (PwDs)** | **`₹6,000`** |

- Cheyutha continues and enhances the earlier **Aasara** pension scheme; eligibility age for old-age pension was lowered over time (*verify current age & amounts*).
- One of the largest direct-benefit social-security nets in the state.

**Rajiv Aarogyasri (health insurance):**
- Cashless treatment for **BPL / white-ration-card families** at empanelled hospitals.
- Coverage ceiling **raised toward `₹10 lakh`** per family under the Congress government's promise (*verify latest official figure*).
- Named after former PM **Rajiv Gandhi**; a pioneering state health-insurance model (launched originally in 2007 in undivided AP).
- Complements the Central **Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY)** — comparison/overlap is a favourite question.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — Cheyutha pays **`₹4,000`** to most categories and **`₹6,000`** to persons with disabilities — the two-tier figure is the single most-asked point.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**4 for most, 6 for special-abled**" (₹4,000 general / ₹6,000 PwD); Aarogyasri = "**Aarogya** = health, up to **₹10 lakh**".

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Cheyutha (pensions) ≠ Aarogyasri (health insurance). And Aarogyasri is a **Telangana/AP** scheme; **Ayushman Bharat** is the **Central** one — don't merge them.

**💡 Example:** A 60-year-old widow beedi-worker draws the `₹4,000` Cheyutha pension monthly, and if hospitalised for a cardiac procedure, Rajiv Aarogyasri covers it cashless up to the ceiling — one household, two guarantees, a classic linked-scheme question.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **The Kakatiyas — Political History**

The dynasty that gave Telangana its golden age, its capital city, and its identity symbol. Today: the political story from feudatory to empire.

**Origins & early rulers:**
- Began as **feudatories** of the Rashtrakutas, then the **Kalyani Chalukyas**; capital first at **Hanamkonda**, later **Orugallu (Warangal)**.
- **Beta Raja I** — early consolidator; **Prola Raja II** — expanded and asserted independence as Chalukya power waned.
- **Rudradeva (Prataparudra I)** — first fully **independent** Kakatiya sovereign; wrote *Nitisara* (Telugu); built the **Thousand Pillar Temple** at Hanamkonda (`1163`).
- **Mahadeva** — died in conflict with the Seunas (Yadavas of Devagiri).

**The peak:**
- **Ganapati Deva** (r. c. `1199–1262`) — longest reign, greatest expansion; unified Telugu lands; issued the **Motupalli Abhaya Sasanam** guaranteeing safety to foreign sea-traders at the port of Motupalli.
- **Rani Rudrama Devi** (r. c. `1262–1289`) — one of India's few women sovereigns; ruled in her own right (titled *Rudradeva Maharaja*); **Marco Polo** praised her administration; died fighting the Kayasthas/Ambadeva.
- **Prataparudra II** (r. c. `1289–1323`) — the **last** Kakatiya; faced repeated Delhi Sultanate invasions (**Malik Kafur**, then **Ulugh Khan/Muhammad-bin-Tughlaq**); **Warangal fell in `1323`**, Prataparudra taken captive (died en route to Delhi).

**Aftermath:** the fall triggered the **Musunuri Nayaks'** resistance and the eventual Vijayanagara-Bahmani contest — but Kakatiya administrative and irrigation legacies endured.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Rani Rudrama Devi** ruled in her own name as a queen-regnant — among the rare women rulers of medieval India, praised by **Marco Polo**.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Peak trio in order: "**G-R-P**": **G**anapati Deva → **R**udrama Devi → **P**rataparudra II (last).

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — **Rudradeva/Prataparudra I** (12th-c. male, first independent king) ≠ **Prataparudra II** (last king, fell 1323). And Warangal fell in **1323**, not 1310 (that was Malik Kafur's earlier raid/tribute).

**💡 Example:** The **Kakatiya Kala Thoranam** (Warangal gateway) built in this era became the emblem on the **Telangana state seal**, and **Mission Kakatiya** revived their tank network — political history feeding directly into modern statehood symbolism.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Capitals: Hanamkonda → Orugallu (Warangal).
- Rudradeva = first independent king; Thousand Pillar Temple 1163.
- Ganapati Deva = Motupalli Abhaya Sasanam; longest reign.
- Rudrama Devi = queen, Marco Polo, married into the Nidadavolu Chalukyas (Virabhadra).
- Prataparudra II = last, captured 1323 by Ulugh Khan.
- Delhi raids: Malik Kafur (1310 tribute), then annexation 1323.

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **Revolutionary Movement — Phase I (Bengal, Punjab & Abroad)**

Alongside the Extremists, a militant underground chose the pistol and the bomb. Phase I (c. `1905–1917`) is dense with names, societies and dates.

**Bengal:**
- **Anushilan Samiti** (Dhaka & Calcutta) and **Jugantar** — the two big secret societies; ideologues **Barindra Kumar Ghosh, Bhupendranath Datta**.
- **Alipore Bomb Case (1908)**: **Khudiram Bose** and **Prafulla Chaki** threw a bomb at Muzaffarpur (aiming at Judge Kingsford); Khudiram was **hanged (1908)**, one of the youngest martyrs; Aurobindo was tried and acquitted.
- **Rash Behari Bose** and **Sachindranath Sanyal** — the **Delhi Conspiracy / Hardinge Bomb Case (1912)** (bomb on Viceroy Hardinge during the Delhi durbar procession).

**Punjab & Maharashtra:**
- **Chapekar brothers** (Pune) assassinated Rand (`1897`) — an early spark.
- **V.D. Savarkar** & **Abhinav Bharat** (Nashik); the **Nasik Conspiracy (Jackson murder, 1909)**.
- **Lala Har Dayal** — a key link to the overseas movement.

**Abroad:**
- **India House, London** — **Shyamji Krishna Varma**; **Madan Lal Dhingra** shot Curzon-Wyllie (`1909`).
- **Ghadar Party (1913, San Francisco)** — founded by **Lala Har Dayal, Sohan Singh Bhakna**; newspaper *Ghadar*; planned the **1915 Ghadar mutiny** (betrayed and crushed); **Kartar Singh Sarabha** hanged.
- **Berlin Committee / Indo-German conspiracy** during WWI.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The **Ghadar Party (1913, San Francisco)** was the great overseas revolutionary organisation; its planned 1915 uprising was crushed but it seeded later militancy.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Bengal's twin societies: "**A-J**" — **A**nushilan & **J**ugantar. Overseas trio: London (India House) → San Francisco (Ghadar) → Berlin (Committee).

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — **Khudiram Bose** (Alipore/Muzaffarpur, 1908) ≠ **Rash Behari Bose** (Hardinge bomb 1912, later INA precursor) ≠ **Subhas Chandra Bose**. Three different "Boses".

**💡 Example:** The **Alipore Bomb Case** made **Khudiram Bose** a household martyr at 18 — his hanging inspired folk songs across Bengal, showing how revolutionary sacrifice fed mass sentiment even where the Congress mainstream stayed moderate.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Anushilan Samiti & Jugantar = Bengal secret societies.
- Khudiram Bose & Prafulla Chaki — Muzaffarpur/Alipore 1908.
- Savarkar — Abhinav Bharat, *The First War of Independence* (book on 1857).
- Madan Lal Dhingra — London, 1909 (Curzon-Wyllie).
- Ghadar Party 1913 San Francisco; *Ghadar* paper; Kartar Singh Sarabha.
- Rash Behari Bose — Hardinge bomb 1912; later helped raise the INA in Asia.

## 📘 Subject 5: Telangana Geography (with Maps)

### Let's learn about **Soils & Agriculture**

![Schematic map of Telangana — regions, rivers and cultural landmarks](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/faizan574/Learning-Python/main/tgpsc-prep/maps/telangana_regions.svg)
*🗺️ Read the map by colour of the land: red chalka over the granite plateau (centre/south), black regur in the north-west, alluvium along the rivers.*

Yesterday's rainfall map explains today's crop map — soil + water = what grows where.

**Major soil types:**

| Soil | Where | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Red / chalka (sandy loam)** | Most widespread — over the granite-gneiss plateau (centre, south) | Millets, groundnut, pulses, castor |
| **Black cotton (regur)** | Adilabad, Nizamabad, parts of the north-west | **Cotton, soybean, turmeric, chillies** |
| **Alluvial** | Godavari & Krishna valleys/deltas | **Paddy** (rice) |
| **Laterite** | Wetter uplands (patches) | Plantation, coarse crops |

**Cropping seasons:**
- **Vaanakalam (Kharif)** — sown with the SW monsoon (Jun–Jul), harvested Oct–Nov: **paddy, cotton, maize, soybean, red gram**.
- **Yasangi (Rabi)** — winter, tank/borewell irrigated: **paddy, maize, groundnut, jowar, bengal gram**.

**Signature crops & GI/commercial notes:**
- **Rice** — the dominant crop; hugely expanded after Kaleshwaram; **Sanna Biyyam (fine rice)** now in ration distribution.
- **Cotton** — Adilabad's white gold; feeds the textile push.
- **Turmeric** — **Nizamabad (Armoor)** belt; farmers' demand for a **Turmeric Board** (now announced by the Centre — *verify status*) was a big incident.
- **Chillies** — Khammam-Warangal; **Warangal's Enumamula** is one of Asia's largest chilli/agri markets.
- **Maize, red gram (kandi)**, and horticulture (mango, sweet orange/**Batayi in Nalgonda**, oil palm expansion) round out the basket.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Red chalka soil is the most widespread** in Telangana; **black regur** (north-west) is the cotton belt; **alluvium** along the rivers grows paddy.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**R-B-A**" soils → "**M-C-P**" crops: **R**ed→**M**illets, **B**lack→**C**otton, **A**lluvial→**P**addy.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Cotton = **black (regur)** soil, **not** red. And Vaanakalam = **Kharif** (monsoon), Yasangi = **Rabi** (winter) — don't swap the local names.

**💡 Example:** Adilabad's black regur + `~1,100 mm` rain = cotton; Nalgonda's red chalka + irrigation from Nagarjunasagar = paddy and sweet-orange orchards. Soil map plus water map answers "why this crop here?" — TGPSC's favourite reasoning question.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Most widespread soil: red chalka; cotton soil: black regur.
- Alluvial along Godavari/Krishna → paddy.
- Vaanakalam = Kharif; Yasangi = Rabi.
- Turmeric = Nizamabad/Armoor (Turmeric Board demand); Chillies = Warangal Enumamula market.
- Post-Kaleshwaram: paddy area surged; Sanna Biyyam in PDS.
- Nalgonda = sweet orange (Batayi); oil palm being promoted statewide.
