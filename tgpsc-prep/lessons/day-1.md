## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **Articles 1 to 4 — The Union and its Territory**

Part I of the Constitution (Articles 1–4) tells us what India is and how its map can be changed. This is a favourite area for TGPSC because Telangana itself was born through these articles!

- **Article 1**: 'India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States.' The word *Union* (not federation) was chosen by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar to show that states have **no right to secede**.
- **Article 2**: Parliament may **admit or establish new states** into the Union (states not part of India earlier, e.g., Sikkim's association).
- **Article 3**: Parliament can **form new states, alter areas, boundaries or names** of existing states by a **simple majority**. The bill needs the **President's prior recommendation**, and the President refers it to the affected state legislature for its **views** — but those views are **not binding**.
- **Article 4**: Laws made under Articles 2 and 3 are **not constitutional amendments** under Article 368, so no special majority is needed.

**💡 Example:** The **Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014** was passed under Articles 3 and 4 to create **Telangana as the 29th state** on 2 June 2014 — even though the AP Assembly rejected the bill, Parliament could still pass it because the state's views are not binding.

## 📘 Subject 2: Telangana Schemes & Important Incidents

### Let's learn about **The Six Guarantees of the Congress Government**

When the Congress government led by CM A. Revanth Reddy took office in Telangana in December 2023, it promised 'Six Guarantees'. These are the backbone of every scheme question in TGPSC now.

- **Mahalakshmi** — support for women: free bus travel, LPG cylinder at ₹500, and financial assistance to women.
- **Rythu Bharosa** — investment support for farmers and farm labourers, plus crop loan waiver.
- **Gruha Jyothi** — free electricity up to **200 units** per month for eligible households.
- **Indiramma Indlu** — housing for the poor and house-site support.
- **Yuva Vikasam** — support for students' education (Vidya Bharosa).
- **Cheyutha** — enhanced pensions for the elderly, widows, and persons with disabilities.
The government began rolling these out from **December 2023** onwards, starting with free bus travel for women.

**💡 Example:** In the very first cabinet decisions after swearing-in (December 2023), the government launched **free travel for women in TGSRTC ordinary buses** (Mahalakshmi) — an example of a guarantee converted into a scheme within days.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **The Satavahanas**

Let's learn about the Satavahanas — the first great dynasty of the Deccan (c. 2nd century BCE to 3rd century CE), whose earliest coins and capital sites lie in today's Telangana.

- **Homeland**: early sites like **Kotalingala** (Jagtial district, on the Godavari) yielded coins of pre-Satavahana and early Satavahana rulers such as **Simuka**, the founder. Later capitals included **Pratishthana (Paithan)** and **Dharanikota/Amaravati**.
- **Greatest ruler**: **Gautamiputra Satakarni** (c. 2nd century CE) — defeated the Saka ruler Nahapana; his mother **Gautami Balasri's Nashik inscription** praises him as 'destroyer of Sakas, Yavanas and Pahlavas'.
- **Administration**: kingdom divided into *aharas*; officials called *amatyas*; land grants to Brahmins and Buddhist monks began under them.
- **Economy**: cotton textiles, inland and overseas **trade with Rome**; lead, copper and potin **coins**; ports like Kalyan and inland towns flourished.
- **Culture**: king **Hala** composed **Gathasaptasati** in Prakrit; patronage to Buddhism — **Amaravati stupa** art reached its peak under them.

**💡 Example:** The **Kotalingala excavations** in Telangana produced coins of Simuka — evidence used to argue that the Satavahanas rose first from the Telangana region, not Maharashtra. TGPSC loves this Telangana-first angle!

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **The Revolt of 1857 — First War of Independence**

Let's learn about the Revolt of 1857 — the first large-scale, organised challenge to British rule, called by V.D. Savarkar the 'First War of Independence'.

- **Causes**: political (Doctrine of Lapse — Satara, Jhansi, Nagpur annexed; Awadh annexed 1856 on 'misgovernance'), economic (ruin of peasants and artisans), social-religious interference, and military grievances.
- **Immediate cause**: the **greased cartridges** of the new Enfield rifle (cow and pig fat) offended both Hindu and Muslim sepoys.
- **Beginning**: **Mangal Pandey** at Barrackpore (29 March 1857); the revolt proper began at **Meerut on 10 May 1857**; sepoys marched to Delhi and proclaimed **Bahadur Shah Zafar** emperor.
- **Leaders/centres**: Delhi — Bakht Khan; Kanpur — Nana Saheb, Tantia Tope; Jhansi — **Rani Lakshmibai**; Lucknow — Begum Hazrat Mahal; Bihar — **Kunwar Singh**; Faizabad — Maulavi Ahmadullah.
- **Results**: **Government of India Act 1858** — Company rule ended, Crown took over; Queen Victoria's Proclamation; army reorganised; Doctrine of Lapse abandoned.
- **In Hyderabad State**: **Turrebaz Khan and Maulvi Allauddin** attacked the British Residency (17 July 1857) — Telangana's own 1857 heroes!

**💡 Example:** **Turrebaz Khan's attack on the Hyderabad Residency** shows TGPSC's pattern of linking national events to Telangana — remember him and Maulvi Allauddin whenever 1857 is asked.

## 📘 Subject 5: Telangana Geography (with Maps)

### Let's learn about **Telangana at a Glance — Location, Extent and Physiography**

Let's learn about where Telangana sits on the map of India, its shape, size and physical divisions — the base layer for every geography question.

![Schematic map of Telangana — regions, rivers and one cultural landmark per region (not to scale)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/faizan574/Learning-Python/main/tgpsc-prep/maps/telangana_regions.svg)
*🗺️ Schematic map of Telangana — regions, rivers and one cultural landmark per region (not to scale)*

- **Location**: a **landlocked** state on the **Deccan Plateau**, roughly between 15°50'–19°55' N latitude and 77°14'–81°19' E longitude.
- **Area**: about **1,12,077 sq km** — the 11th largest state; **33 districts** at present (10 at formation in 2014, reorganised in 2016 and after).
- **Neighbours**: **Maharashtra** (north-northwest), **Chhattisgarh** (northeast), **Karnataka** (west), **Andhra Pradesh** (south and east).
- **Relief**: average elevation around 500 m, sloping broadly from west to east/southeast; the **Godavari** drains the north, the **Krishna** the south.
- **Highest point**: **Doli Gutta** (~965 m) on the Mulugu–Chhattisgarh border ranges.
- **Physiographic zones**: the eastern hilly belt (extensions of the Eastern Ghats — Nallamala in the south), the central plateau (Telangana plateau of granite-gneiss), and river valleys/plains along the Godavari and Krishna.

**💡 Example:** Look at the map: every region carries a cultural tag — Warangal's Kakatiya Thoranam, Khammam's Bhadrachalam, Mahabubnagar's Alampur. TGPSC rarely asks 'plain' geography; it asks geography **through** culture, which is why our maps annotate both together.
