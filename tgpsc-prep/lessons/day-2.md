## 📘 Subject 1: Indian Constitution & Polity

### Let's learn about **Making of the Constitution & the Constituent Assembly**

Before the articles, know the workshop where they were forged — TGPSC regularly asks dates, names and numbers from the Constituent Assembly.

**The demand grows:**

1. `1934` — **M.N. Roy** first floats the idea of a Constituent Assembly.
2. `1935` — Indian National Congress officially demands one.
3. `1940` — **August Offer** accepts the principle; `1942` **Cripps Mission** offers a post-war body.
4. `1946` — **Cabinet Mission Plan** finally creates it.

**Composition under the Cabinet Mission Plan:**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Total strength | `389` (British India `296` + Princely States `93`) |
| Election | **Indirect** — by Provincial Legislative Assemblies |
| Voting | Single transferable vote, communal categories (General/Muslim/Sikh) |
| After Partition | Strength reduced to `299` |

**Key dates & office-bearers:**

- First meeting: `9 December 1946` — **Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha** interim President (Muslim League boycotted).
- `11 December 1946` — **Dr. Rajendra Prasad** elected permanent President.
- **B.N. Rau** — Constitutional Advisor; **H.C. Mookherjee** and **V.T. Krishnamachari** — Vice-Presidents.
- `13 December 1946` — Nehru moves the **Objectives Resolution** (adopted `22 January 1947`) → becomes the **Preamble**.
- **Drafting Committee** formed `29 August 1947` — chairman **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar** + six members (N. Gopalaswamy Ayyangar, Alladi Krishnaswami Ayyar, K.M. Munshi, Syed Mohammad Saadulla, N. Madhava Rau, T.T. Krishnamachari).
- Constitution **adopted `26 November 1949`** (Constitution Day / Samvidhan Divas); fully in force **`26 January 1950`** — the date honouring the `1930` **Purna Swaraj** pledge.
- Time taken: `2 years 11 months 18 days`; `11` sessions.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The Assembly was **not directly elected** by the people, yet it included every shade of opinion — a favourite "criticism vs reality" analytical question.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**S**inha **S**tarted, **P**rasad **P**resided, **A**mbedkar **A**uthored" — the three names in chronological order.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Drafting Committee = `7` members **including** the chairman. Also don't confuse *adopted* (`26 Nov 1949`) with *commenced* (`26 Jan 1950`).

**💡 Example:** Representatives of **Hyderabad State** could not join the Assembly initially because the Nizam stayed out of the merger process — its seats among the princely `93` were filled only after political changes in `1948-49`, connecting this topic straight to Telangana's own story.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- `9 Dec 1946` first sitting — in the Constitution Hall (now Samvidhan Sadan).
- Objectives Resolution = philosophical soul → Preamble.
- Ambedkar = "Father of the Indian Constitution"; called Article 32 "the heart and soul".
- `26 November` celebrated as **Constitution Day** since 2015.
- Elephant 🐘 was the Assembly's seal/symbol.
- The Assembly also served as the **first provisional Parliament** till 1952.

## 📘 Subject 2: Telangana Schemes & Important Incidents

### Let's learn about **Mahalakshmi Scheme — the Deep Dive**

Yesterday you met the Six Guarantees; today we dissect the flagship women-welfare guarantee that TGPSC can question from five different angles.

**The three components:**

| Component | Benefit | Start |
|---|---|---|
| Free bus travel | Zero-fare travel for **women & transgender persons** in **TGSRTC ordinary buses** (Palle Velugu, Express) **within Telangana** | `9 December 2023` |
| Subsidised LPG | Cylinder refills at **`₹500`** for eligible (white ration card) households | early `2024` |
| Financial assistance | Promised monthly support to women (rollout status — *verify latest official figure*) | — |

**How free travel works:**
- The conductor issues a **"zero ticket"** — fare printed but not collected — creating an auditable record.
- Government **reimburses TGSRTC** based on zero-ticket totals; official statements have cited **crores of free journeys per month** and totals in the **hundreds of crores of trips** since launch (*verify latest official figure before the exam*).
- **Not covered**: AC/luxury services (Rajdhani, Garuda etc.), inter-state journeys.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — Eligibility rests on being a **woman or transgender resident of Telangana**; identity proof is shown on board. Out-of-state women travellers are **not** covered.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Mahalakshmi = **T-B-G**: **T**ravel free, **B**us ordinary, **G**as at ₹500.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — The `₹500` cylinder is under **Mahalakshmi**, but free power `200 units` is **Gruha Jyothi** — MCQs love swapping these two scheme names.

**Why it matters (analysis angle):**
- Boosts women's **mobility & workforce participation**; savings for poor households.
- Raised TGSRTC occupancy sharply — buses became crowded, prompting fleet expansion.
- Fiscal cost: government reimburses the corporation monthly — a standard "welfare vs fiscal burden" debate question.

**💡 Example:** A domestic worker commuting daily from Medchal to Secunderabad by Express bus saves roughly `₹1,500–2,000` a month — equivalent to several days' wages — while the same journey by Metro or AC bus still costs full fare. That contrast is exactly how TGPSC frames application questions.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Launch `9 Dec 2023` — two days after the government took oath.
- Implementing agency: **TGSRTC** (note the rename from TSRTC).
- Zero ticket = reimbursement accounting device.
- Transgender persons explicitly included.
- Covers ordinary services **within** the state only.
- ₹500 LPG tied to white ration cards / eligible households.

## 📘 Subject 3: Telangana History

### Let's learn about **Pre-Satavahana Telangana — Megaliths, Asmaka and the First Towns**

Before Simuka's coins, Telangana already had astronomy-savvy megalith builders and the **only mahajanapada of the South**. This "before the beginning" topic is a TGPSC favourite.

**Megalithic culture (c. `1000–300 BCE`):**
- Iron-using, cattle-and-agriculture communities who raised **burial monuments**: cairns, cists, dolmens and **menhirs**; typical pottery = **black-and-red ware**.
- **Mudumal (Narayanpet district)** — India's largest concentration of **menhirs**; alignments track solstice sunrises, making it an ancient **astronomical observatory**; added to **UNESCO's Tentative List** (*verify current status*).
- Megalithic burials are found across Telangana — the direct ancestors of the early historic settlements.

**Asmaka (Assaka) Mahajanapada (c. `6th century BCE`):**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Status | One of the **16 mahajanapadas** — the **only one in South India** |
| Location | On the **Godavari**, covering parts of present Telangana–Maharashtra border region |
| Capital | **Potali / Podana** — identified with **Bodhan** (Nizamabad district) |
| Sources | Buddhist texts (Anguttara Nikaya), Panini, Jataka tales |

**First urban centres (early historic, pre/early Satavahana):**
- **Kotalingala** (Jagtial, on the Godavari) — coins of local pre-Satavahana rulers (e.g., *Gobhada*, *Samagopa*) and of **Simuka** — evidence of a mint town before the empire.
- **Dhulikatta** (Karimnagar) — fortified town with a **Buddhist stupa**; fine gateway remains.
- **Peddabankur** (Karimnagar) — brick structures, wells, punch-marked and Roman coins — a trading settlement.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Asmaka's capital Bodhan** puts a mahajanapada capital inside modern Telangana — the state's claim to 2,600 years of urban political history.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — "**M-A-K**" timeline: **M**egaliths → **A**smaka → **K**otalingala — stones, state, coins.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Don't write Amaravati/Dharanikota here — those are Krishna-valley sites; the pre-Satavahana Telangana cluster is on the **Godavari** (Kotalingala-Dhulikatta-Peddabankur).

**💡 Example:** At **Mudumal**, one aligned row of menhirs frames the rising sun exactly on the solstices — villagers still call a stone *Niluvu Rayi*. One site, three exam subjects: history (megaliths), geography (Krishna basin uplands), culture (living tradition).

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- 16 mahajanapadas → only southern one = **Asmaka**; capital **Bodhan (Podana)**.
- Mudumal = menhirs + archaeoastronomy + UNESCO tentative list.
- Kotalingala = pre-Satavahana **coins**; Dhulikatta = **stupa**; Peddabankur = **trade town**.
- Megalithic pottery = black-and-red ware; metal = **iron**.
- All three early towns sit in the **Godavari valley**.

## 📘 Subject 4: Indian National Movement

### Let's learn about **Pre-Congress Associations & Socio-Religious Reform Movements**

The INC (1885) did not appear from nowhere — a century of reformers and early associations prepared the ground. Expect 1-2 questions from this feeder topic.

**Socio-religious reform (the awakening):**

| Movement | Founder & Year | Signature |
|---|---|---|
| **Brahmo Samaj** | Raja Rammohan Roy, `1828` | Against sati (abolished `1829`, Bentinck), idolatry; monotheism |
| **Young Bengal** | H.L.V. Derozio, 1820s | Radical rationalism |
| **Prarthana Samaj** | Atmaram Pandurang, `1867` (M.G. Ranade) | Reform within Hinduism, Maharashtra |
| **Arya Samaj** | Swami Dayananda Saraswati, `1875` | "**Back to the Vedas**"; *Satyarth Prakash*; shuddhi |
| **Theosophical Society** | Blavatsky & Olcott `1875`; Adyar HQ `1882` | **Annie Besant**; revival of Indian pride |
| **Ramakrishna Mission** | Swami Vivekananda, `1897` | Service as worship; Chicago address `1893` |
| **Aligarh Movement** | Sir Syed Ahmad Khan | MAO College `1875`; modern education for Muslims |
| **Satyashodhak Samaj** | Jyotiba Phule, `1873` | Anti-caste, education for women & bahujans |
| **SNDP Yogam** | Sri Narayana Guru, `1903` | "One caste, one religion, one God for man" |

**Early political associations (the rehearsal):**

1. `1838` — **Landholders' Society** (Calcutta) — first organised body.
2. `1851` — **British Indian Association**.
3. `1866` — **East India Association**, London — **Dadabhai Naoroji**.
4. `1870` — **Poona Sarvajanik Sabha** — Ranade's circle.
5. `1876` — **Indian Association**, Calcutta — **Surendranath Banerjea** & Ananda Mohan Bose; led the civil-service age-limit agitation; convened the **Indian National Conference** (`1883, 1885`).
6. `1884` — **Madras Mahajana Sabha**; `1885` — **Bombay Presidency Association**.

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — The **Indian Association (1876)** is called the *forerunner of the INC* — its 1885 National Conference merged into the newborn Congress.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Reformer pairs: "**R**oy **R**ejected sati, **D**ayananda **D**emanded Vedas, **P**hule **P**ushed equality, **V**ivekananda **V**oiced India abroad."

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Arya Samaj (1875, Bombay) and Theosophical Society (1875, New York) share the year — examiners swap their founders. Also, Brahmo *Sabha* (1828) was later renamed Brahmo *Samaj*.

**💡 Example:** In **Hyderabad State**, the **Arya Samaj** became a real political force by the 1930s — its `1938-39` satyagraha against the Nizam's restrictions fed directly into Telangana's own freedom struggle: a reform movement turning into resistance.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Sati abolition: `1829`, Regulation XVII, Bentinck + Rammohan Roy.
- Widow Remarriage Act `1856` — Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar's campaign.
- Naoroji's East India Association = London lobbying arm.
- Surendranath Banerjea = "Indian Burke", twice INC president later.
- Annie Besant: Theosophy → Home Rule (1916) → INC president (1917, first woman).
- Phule's book *Gulamgiri* (1873).

India's second-longest river is Telangana's northern lifeline. Read the map first: entry at the west, the Pranahita boost mid-way, exit past Bhadrachalam.

**The river:**
- Rises at **Trimbakeshwar** (Brahmagiri hills, Nashik, Maharashtra); total length about `1,465 km` — the **largest peninsular river**, hence "**Dakshina Ganga**" (also called *Vriddha Ganga*).
- Enters Telangana at **Kandakurthi** (Renjal mandal, **Nizamabad**) — itself a *Triveni sangamam* of Godavari-**Manjira**-**Haridra**.
- Flows south-east along the state's northern flank and leaves after **Bhadrachalam** towards the AP delta.

**Tributaries in/along Telangana:**

| Bank | Tributary | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Right | **Manjira** | Longest right-bank tributary; carries **Nizamsagar** & **Singur** projects |
| Right | **Maner** | Karimnagar's river; Mid & Lower Maner dams |
| Left | **Pranahita** | Combined waters of Wardha + Wainganga + Penganga — the **largest tributary** by flow |
| Left | **Indravati, Sabari (with Sileru)** | Join along the eastern stretch |
| — | **Kadem, Kinnerasani** | Smaller Telangana tributaries with their own reservoirs |

**Projects strung along it (west → east):**

1. **Nizamsagar** (`1931`, on Manjira) — the Nizam-era classic, oldest major project.
2. **Sriram Sagar Project (SRSP)** at **Pochampadu** — "lifeline of north Telangana".
3. **Kaddam (Kadem) project**.
4. **Sripada Yellampalli** — supplies irrigation + Hyderabad drinking water.
5. **Kaleshwaram LIS** — barrages at **Medigadda (Lakshmi)**, Annaram, Sundilla; lifts Godavari water at the **Pranahita confluence**; among the world's largest lift schemes.
6. **Sammakka Sagar (Tupakulagudem)** and **Sitarama LIS**, **Devadula LIS** — the newer eastern lifts.

**Cultural thread on the banks:** Kandakurthi triveni → **Basara** Gnana Saraswati → **Dharmapuri** Narasimha → **Kaleshwaram** Mukteswara (Triveni) → **Bhadrachalam** Sita Ramachandra. **Godavari Pushkaralu** comes every `12` years (last held `2015`).

> 💎 **KEY FACT** — **Pranahita is the largest tributary** of the Godavari, which is why Kaleshwaram's main barrage (Medigadda) sits just below that confluence.

> 🧠 **MEMORY HOOK** — Entry to exit chant: "**K**andakurthi **B**asara **S**RSP **Y**ellampalli **K**aleshwaram **B**hadrachalam" — *KB-SY-KB*.

> ⚠️ **TRAP ALERT** — Nizamsagar is **not on the Godavari main stem** — it's on the **Manjira**. Likewise Singur (Manjira) supplies Hyderabad, not SRSP.

**💡 Example:** When TGPSC asks "arrange Godavari projects from upstream to downstream", the map answers instantly: SRSP → Kaddam → Yellampalli → Kaleshwaram barrages → Sammakka Sagar — visual learning beats rote lists.

**🎯 Exam Pointers:**
- Origin Trimbakeshwar; length ~`1,465 km`; largest peninsular river.
- Telangana entry: **Kandakurthi**, Nizamabad.
- Largest tributary: **Pranahita**; longest right-bank: **Manjira**.
- Nizamsagar (1931) = Manjira; SRSP = Pochampadu.
- Kaleshwaram's first/main barrage: **Medigadda (Lakshmi barrage)**.
- Pushkaralu cycle: 12 years; Godavari's = when Jupiter enters Simha (Leo).
